High-Skilled Immigration Creates Jobs

Tweet
Q: Hey, I’ve been hearing a lot about H-1B visas lately, but what exactly are they?
A: The H-1B visa is the most common visa awarded to “high-skilled” immigrants who want to work in the U.S. Every year, the government awards 65,000 H-1B visas to immigrants with bachelor’s degrees and 20,000 H-1B visas to immigrants with master’s degrees. But that’s nowhere close to meeting the demand: in 2014, the government received over 172,000 H-1B visa applications.
Q: But that’s good, right? If we let in too many immigrants, won’t they take all our jobs?
A: NO! The opposite is actually true. In fact, for every 100 H-1B visas the government awards to immigrants, an additional 183 American jobs are created.
Q: But… wait. That can’t be right. When immigrants fill open jobs, they’re obviously taking a job from an American.
A: The thing is, high-skilled immigrants create jobs in two ways: they support workers across the economy simply by spending their paychecks, and they help their companies to succeed so they can hire even more employees. Just think about it – every time an immigrant buys a house, she helps a realtor stay in business. And if a local startup can find just the right engineer to help them rake in an extra million in billings, they can hire a ton more employees.
Q: Hmm… okay. That makes sense. Maybe there’s something to immigration reform, after all. A: Absolutely! And we’d better hurry up: we’ve lost out on over $24 billion in tax revenue since 2013.
